About Us

Dunwell Methodist Sunday School is a dedicated school that has been set up to;

  • Bible Study Lessons:
    Structured lessons exploring different books, themes, or characters from the Bible. Integration of Methodist teachings and traditions.
  • Worship and Singing: Incorporating Methodist hymns and songs into worship sessions. Encouraging active participation in singing and praise.
  • Prayer Time: Dedicated time for prayer, including Methodist prayer traditions and practices. Teaching the importance of prayer in the Methodist faith.
  • Crafts and Arts: Creative projects related to Methodist teachings and values. Crafts that reflect Methodist symbols and history.
  • Games and Activities: Educational games that reinforce Methodist beliefs and values. Interactive activities that engage participants in a fun and informative manner.
  • Memorization: Focusing on memorizing key Methodist scriptures and teachings. Memorization challenges and rewards for participants.
  • Special Events: Celebrating Methodist holidays and events. Organizing special events that align with Methodist traditions
  • Mission Projects: Engaging in community service projects that reflect Methodist principles of social justice. Connecting mission work with Methodist outreach and compassion.
  • Celebrations and Graduations: Recognizing achievements and milestones in a manner that reflects Methodist values. Graduation ceremonies for different age groups within the Sunday School.
  • Guest Speakers: Inviting Methodist clergy or members to share their experiences and insights. Emphasizing Methodist history and its impact on the community.
  • Drama and Skits: Incorporating skits and dramas that portray Methodist stories or parables. Encouraging creativity in presenting Methodist teachings.
  • Parenting Involment: Facilitating parent-teacher meetings to discuss Methodist education and values. Involving parents in special events and activities
  • Sunday School Outings: Organizing outings or field trips that align with Methodist values. Connecting nature or historical trips with Methodist teachings.
